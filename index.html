A Kettle of Fish

A "kettle of fish" is an idiom referring to a situation, often a troublesome or awkward one. The phrase likely traces back to 18th-century Scotland, where a riverside picnic featuring freshly caught salmon boiled in a kettle was itself called a "kettle of fish."

Today the expression usually appears as "a fine kettle of fish" or "a different kettle of fish" — the first lamenting a mess, the second marking something as an entirely separate matter.
